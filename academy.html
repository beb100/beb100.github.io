Academy Life & Preparation

Life at the Pennsylvania State Police Academy

The PSP Academy is designed to challenge cadets physically, mentally, and emotionally. Training is rigorous and helps develop the skills necessary for law enforcement duties across the Commonwealth.

How to Prepare for the Academy

Before arriving at the Academy, cadets should develop strong fitness habits, discipline, and a healthy lifestyle. Below are some recommended goals and tips:

Pre-Arrival Fitness Goals

  • Run 4 miles continuously at a pace between 8–11 min/mile.
  • Run up a hill or incline for 100 yards without stopping.
  • Perform 30 consecutive push-ups (no breaks).
  • Perform 30 consecutive sit-ups (no breaks).
  • Do 8 consecutive pull-ups (no breaks).
  • Bench-press roughly 85% of your body weight.

Healthy Habits & Training Suggestions

  • Adopt a nutritious diet — eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat/non-fat dairy; avoid candy, desserts, soft drinks, and alcohol.
  • Maintain consistent sleep, eating, and exercise routines.
  • Start walking if new to exercise, then gradually build up to running; include hills, stairs, or hiking.
  • Perform cardiovascular workouts (running, cycling, swimming, etc.) and include interval training or cross-training for endurance.
  • Incorporate strength training or circuit workouts to build a solid strength base.

Bottom line: Arrive at the Academy both mentally and physically prepared to meet the challenges ahead.
